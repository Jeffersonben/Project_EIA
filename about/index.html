1. FOUNDING
The EIA, founded in 1993 by Mr. A. Sasikaran and 1992 G.C.E Advanced Level students, aimed to aid civil war refugees through teaching, exams, and sports. Initially 30 members, Mr. Mathiyalahan was the first president, and Mr. Shiyamasundaram and Mr. Jayaruban held secretary and treasurer roles. Mr. A. Sasikaran and Mr. Thayanandan were founding patrons, shaping EIA's activities.

2. MEMBERSHIP
From 1993 to 1995, the members were A/L students of Batticaloa district who had sat for the examination. From 1996 the membership consisted of the students who succeeded in the A/L examination gaining eligibility to study engineering and medicine. Since 2005 engineering and medical graduates were also included in the membership to further improve and expand the activities of EIA.

3. MANAGEMENT STRUCTURE
From 1993 to 2005, EIA's management comprised a president, vice president, secretary, treasurer, and committee members, typically recent A/L graduates. Senior members provided guidance. The president oversaw all activities, the vice-president acted as a backup and handled major tasks, the secretary managed documentation and liaised with educational institutions, and the treasurer handled finances. In 2005, as EIA expanded, a Senior Executive Committee was established to oversee new projects and maintain ongoing operations.

4. PRESENT MANAGEMENT STRUCTURE
Once the Senior Executive Committee has been established, the EIA management structure consists of two major hierarchical levels; Senior Executive Committee and Junior Committee. The Senior Executive Committee is responsible for planning the activities, formulating the annual budget, maintaining the resources, and ensuring proper documentation of activities over time, while the Junior Committee is responsible for executing the planned activities.

5. GENERAL ACTIVITIES
Initially, EIA aimed to enhance education for grades six to O/L, but in 1999, it shifted its focus to A/L education, recognizing its critical role in students' futures. EIA's notable achievements in A/L education include securing an additional university entrance quota for all A/L stream students in Batticaloa district. By protesting from 2003 to 2004, Batticaloa was declared an educationally backward district. EIA now concentrates on increasing university admissions and merit passes in A/L exams for Batticaloa and Ampara districts through activities such as quiz competitions, practice exams, model exams, and seminars.
